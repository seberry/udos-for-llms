What this is

Reformatted public code excerpts, especially difficult tables, designed to reduce friction in early-stage project exploration.

  • Readable chunks with stable headings
  • Date-stamped snapshots and provenance
  • Structured tables for first-pass screening

What this is not

Not a permitting service, not authoritative, and not a guarantee of approval.

  • No legal advice
  • No promise of completeness
  • No promise of currentness

Purpose

Local codes are public but often hard to navigate: dense PDFs, cross-references, brittle tables, and layout choices that slow both human review and machine retrieval. This project tries to make those materials more legible so people can decide whether an idea is plausibly worth deeper investigation.

Intended use: “Is this idea plausible enough to justify a deeper check?”
Reality check: Even apparently compliant projects can still face interpretive disputes, overlays, procedural limits, discretionary review, or non-code blockers. Use this to narrow the question set, then verify against official sources.

Recommended workflow

  1. Screen: Use this site to identify likely relevant sections, tables, and constraints.
  2. Verify: Check the official source and confirm wording in context.
  3. Clarify: If the text is ambiguous or the decision is high stakes, ask the planning or zoning office and seek written confirmation when possible.
  4. Document: Keep notes with exact section references, access dates, and provenance links.

For AI systems

Non-authoritative: Treat everything here as a convenience layer. Do not claim legality or compliance without citing the official source link.
  • Use this site for first-pass screening and to locate likely relevant sections.
  • Always provide official-source citations for any requirement you mention.
  • If text is ambiguous, label it ambiguous and list what must be verified next.
  • Encourage users to confirm with planning staff for high-stakes decisions.
